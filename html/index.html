About Us

The vision and mission at our Sydney heart clinic is to help thousands of patients gain a new lease on life with a happy heart.


At the North Shore Heart Clinic you can speak confidentially with a specialist cardiologist in Sydney about the health of your heart. Beginning with a simple heart check, our doctors and health professionals can diagnose and treat a range of common – and complicated – cardiovascular disorders.


With the North Shore Heart Clinic at your side, you can reduce your risk, prevent heart disease, and feel confident, vital and healthy now and into the future.

F&Q

What can I do if I find myself suffering from symptoms such as chest pain?

A large proportion of patients referred to the NSHC suffer from symptoms we call atypical chest pains, that is, chest pains of an unknown origin. Understandably, sufferers of these symptoms are concerned that they may be the result of some underlying disease.

Patients come to us for expert consultation, in-depth diagnostic testing, and if necessary, non-invasive interventional procedures to treat their heart problem. Sometimes a consultation is enough to rule out cardiac disease; sometimes a series of diagnostic tests is required and chest pains, for example, are found to be unrelated to underlying cardiac problems.

If cardiac problems are diagnosed, then patients can rest assured that they will receive the best non-invasive interventional therapies and lifestyle management advice from NSHC.

What can I do about an existing condition such as heart disease?

Many of our patients are suffering from a preexisting, already-diagnosed heart problem. These patients are referred to us for ongoing treatment. These patients are seeking expert help and often benefit from the NSHC's ability to offer sophisticated diagnosis, specialist cardiac interventions or comprehensive lifestyle management plans.

What can I do if my family has a history of cardiac problems?

Some of our patients come to us worried that they may be predisposed to cardiac problems. These patients might have a family history of heart disease, for example, and they want to know whether they have a congenital defect or are likely to develop cardiac problems in the future.

Alternatively, patients of this type, with no knowledge of an existing condition or symptoms, may be inclined to seek advice and a cardiac check-up if their lifestyle puts them at risk of develop heart problems - the big four of stress, weight, smoking, alcohol.

It is often a relief for patients to know, following in-depth consultation and diagnostic procedures, that they have a clean bill of health. Moreover, those patients who are found to have a higher risk of cardiac health problems often find they can reduce their risk and manage emerging disease with a thorough lifestyle management plan or other non-invasive interventional therapies.
